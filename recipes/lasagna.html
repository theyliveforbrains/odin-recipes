Lasagna Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried lasagna
  • 2 pounds ricotta
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 5 to 6 cups meat sauce
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano or Pecorino Romano
  • 1 large bunch basil, leaves removed, rinsed and dried
  • 12 ounces fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced

Cooking Instructions

  1. In large pot, bring 4 quarts salted water to a boil.
  2. Add a few pieces of lasagna and cook, stirring gently, until tender but slightly underdone.
  3. Scoop the pasta out of the water with a sieve, and cool it in a bowl of cold water.
  4. Cook remaining pasta.
  5. When cool enough to handle, lay the lasagna out flat on lint-free (not terry cloth) kitchen towels.
  6. In a bowl, beat the ricotta and salt and pepper to taste; set aside.
  7. Spread a thin layer of the sauce in a 9-inch-by-13-inch baking pan.
  8. Place a few sheets of lasagna in the pan in a single layer, overlapping slightly.
  9. Spread evenly with about one-third of ricotta mixture and sprinkle with 2 tablespoons grated cheese.
  10. Top with a layer of basil leaves and another of mozzarella slices. Make two more layers the same way.
  11. Top with a final layer of lasagna, sauce and remaining grated cheese.

The lasagna can be made ahead to this point. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate several hours or overnight.

Baking Instructions

  1. Place a rack in the center of the oven. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
  2. Bake lasagna for 45 minutes.
  3. If lasagna is browning too much, cover it loosely with foil.
  4. Bake 15 minutes more (longer if it has been refrigerated), or until top is browned and sauce is bubbling around edges.
  5. Let stand 15 minutes.
  6. Cut into squares and serve.
